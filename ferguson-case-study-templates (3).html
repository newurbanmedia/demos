Hydraulic Press Rebuild for Memphis Food Processing Facility

Emergency hydraulic system rebuild restored full production in under 48 hours

Ferguson Industrial Co. diagnosed and rebuilt a failing hydraulic press system at a Memphis food packaging plant, eliminating recurring pressure loss and restoring the line to full output before a critical shipping deadline.

Featured Project Photo
Confidential
Food Processing
Memphis, Tennessee
Packaging & Distribution Plant
Now Under Maintenance Agreement

The Problem

A 200-ton hydraulic press on the facility's primary packaging line began losing pressure intermittently during peak production shifts. The plant's maintenance team replaced seals twice in 10 days, but pressure drops continued. Each failure shut the line down for 2 to 4 hours. With a major retail shipment deadline 5 days out, the plant manager needed the root cause found and fixed.

What Was at Stake

The packaging line accounted for roughly 60% of the facility's weekly output. Every unplanned shutdown cost the plant an estimated $8,500 in lost production per hour. A missed shipment deadline carried contractual penalties and risked the client's standing with a national retail partner.

The Approach

Ferguson's field service team arrived on-site within 4 hours of the initial call. Rather than replacing seals again, the team pulled the hydraulic cylinder for full inspection at Ferguson's Olive Branch shop. Diagnostic testing revealed a scored cylinder bore and a hairline crack in the manifold block, both invisible during in-place seal swaps.

Scope of Work

The team re-honed the cylinder bore to spec, fabricated a replacement manifold block using CNC machining, replaced all seals and fittings with Parker components, and pressure-tested the rebuilt assembly to 3,200 PSI before reinstallation. A two-person crew returned to the plant for reinstallation and ran the press through a full production cycle to verify performance under load.

The Result

The rebuilt press was back online 43 hours after the initial service call. The line met its shipping deadline with a day to spare. In the 90 days following the rebuild, the press logged zero unplanned shutdowns. The plant operations director signed an ongoing maintenance agreement within 30 days of the completed repair.
